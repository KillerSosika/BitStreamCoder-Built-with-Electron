How Encoding Works

Encoding transforms data into a format suitable for transmission or storage. Below are common encoding methods:

  • NRZ: Direct representation of binary signals.
  • NRZI: Changes state on 1, remains on 0.
  • AMI: Alternating marks to reduce DC bias.
  • Manchester: XOR of data with clock signal.
  • Bipolar: Uses three voltage levels (+, -, 0).
  • 2B1Q: Two-bit symbols mapped to four voltage levels.
